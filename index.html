ICT 500 Reflection

Critical and Creative Thinking

Reflections on learning from ICT 500 course

Self Reflection on Learning

Taking ICT 500 has genuinely changed how I approach technology problems. Before this course, I'd often jump straight into coding without really thinking through the problem. Now, I find myself naturally breaking down challenges into smaller pieces and considering different angles before diving in. The Smart Helmet HUD project was a perfect example‚Äîinstead of just building features, I learned to step back and critically evaluate what riders actually need for safety, which led to much better design decisions.

What really clicked for me was understanding that there's a method to problem-solving, not just trial and error. The frameworks we learned gave me actual tools I could use when things got complicated. When I was coordinating the team and dividing up tasks, these thinking techniques helped me see which pieces depended on others and how to organize everything logically. It's like I finally have a proper toolkit instead of just improvising every time I face a challenge.

One thing that surprised me was how much the course pushed us to think creatively while still being analytical. I used to think these were opposites, but working with my team on the Figma designs showed me they actually work together. We'd brainstorm wild ideas, then use critical thinking to figure out which ones would actually work. This balance between imagination and practical analysis has become something I try to bring to every project now, and it definitely makes the solutions better.

Self Reflection on Project Goals

Honestly, tracking progress used to stress me out because I never knew if we were actually on track or just busy. ICT 500 taught me to set clear, specific goals that you can actually measure‚Äînot just vague "make progress" statements. For the helmet project, I started doing regular check-ins with the team where we'd look at what we planned versus what we actually did. This simple practice, which came straight from the course's analytical methods, made such a difference. We could see our Figma designs evolving week by week and knew exactly where we stood.

The biggest help was learning how to break down responsibilities clearly. Before, I'd assign tasks but people weren't always sure what I meant or what the priority was. Using the structured thinking methods from class, I got better at explaining not just what needed doing, but why it mattered and how it connected to everything else. Even when we were all juggling other classes and I was working full-time, having this clear structure kept us moving forward. The creative side of the course also helped us figure out innovative ways to match the helmet features with what riders actually want.

Not everything went smoothly though. Working full-time while studying meant I had to get creative with time management, which was honestly tough. There were moments I felt overwhelmed trying to balance everything. But those struggles actually forced me to use the adaptive thinking strategies we learned‚Äîadjusting plans when needed, prioritizing ruthlessly, and not being afraid to ask for help. Looking back, these challenges taught me that hitting your goals isn't just about perfect planning; it's about being flexible and resilient when things get complicated.

Self Reflection on Group Dynamics

Working with my team taught me that everyone thinks differently, and that's actually a strength when you handle it right. ICT 500 really emphasized how different perspectives make solutions stronger, and I saw this firsthand. Some team members were great at spotting potential problems, while others came up with creative features I'd never have thought of. My job was basically to make sure we had space for both the critical thinkers and the creative brainstormers to contribute. When everyone felt heard and valued, the quality of our work just naturally improved.

There were definitely rough patches, especially during busy weeks when everyone was drowning in deadlines. Communication got harder when we were all stressed and tired. But what helped was using the problem-solving approaches from class‚Äîwhen issues came up, we'd identify them clearly and figure out solutions together instead of letting frustration build up. My teammates were honestly great about this. They'd offer support when someone was struggling but also weren't afraid to question ideas respectfully. That combination of being supportive but still analytical is something the course really stressed, and seeing it work in practice was pretty cool.

The whole experience made me realize that good teamwork isn't just about being nice to each other‚Äîit's about building real trust and holding each other accountable. When we split up tasks fairly and kept communication open about what we were doing, things just ran smoother. We avoided the drama that sometimes happens in group projects because everyone knew what was expected and felt their contributions mattered. It showed me that the thinking methods ICT 500 teaches aren't just for solving technical problems; they actually help people work together better too.

Self Reflection on Critical Thinking & Other Reflections

I think one thing I'm decent at is organizing chaos‚Äîtaking a messy situation and finding some structure in it. ICT 500 gave me better tools for this by teaching actual methods for sorting through information and weighing different options. When I was managing the project timeline and making sure everyone's work fit together, these frameworks were super helpful. It felt good to realize that my natural tendency to organize could be backed up by proper analytical thinking instead of just gut feeling.

But the course also made me face some uncomfortable truths about my weaknesses. I'm terrible at staying on top of communication when I'm overwhelmed, and juggling work with university really exposed that. There were times when I should have responded to the group faster or been more present in discussions, but I was just too stretched thin. The course's focus on self-reflection actually helped me see this pattern more clearly, even if I haven't fully solved it yet. At least now I'm more aware when it's happening and can try to catch myself.

What really satisfied me was watching our idea evolve from rough concepts into something that actually looked professional. Using the critical and creative thinking methods we learned, we went from "wouldn't a smart helmet be cool?" to a properly thought-out design with real features that make sense. Seeing that progression validated everything the course taught‚Äîthat you need both the analytical side and the creative side working together. It's not enough to just have ideas; you need to think them through carefully too.

Moving forward, I know I need to work on stress management and not letting myself get too overwhelmed. The course taught us about sustainable approaches to problem-solving, but honestly implementing that when life gets hectic is still something I'm learning. I want to get better at using the prioritization techniques we learned so I don't burn out trying to do everything at once. The thinking skills from ICT 500 will definitely help with future projects, but I also need to apply them to taking care of myself better in the process.
